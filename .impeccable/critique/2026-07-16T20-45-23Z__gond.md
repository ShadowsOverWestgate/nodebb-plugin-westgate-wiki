## Overall Impression

This is a real product surface, not slop: disciplined token contract, wiki-native vocabulary, zero forum chrome inside the article. The gothic Westgate voice lands immediately. What holds it back is structural: the page's core promise — reading-first — is undermined by unmeasured prose, a duplicated title, and the reader's main aid (Contents) hidden behind a drawer while an editor-flavored FAB dock floats permanently, even for guests. The single biggest opportunity is fixing the reading column.

## What's Working

1. **The token contract is real discipline.** `wiki-article-body.css` routes every prose element through `--wiki-prose-*` with Bootstrap fallbacks; the article surface would survive a theme swap. Rare rigor for a forum plugin.
2. **Byline as provenance, not forum chrome.** "Last edited by X … created by Y" with timeago and user links delivers wiki trust signals with zero avatar/reply clutter.
3. **Drawer accessibility plumbing.** `aria-expanded`, `aria-controls`, labeled backdrop button, `hidden` management, visible focus rings on tabs.

## Priority Issues

1. **[P0] Unmeasured prose line length.** `wiki.css:8` opts out of any measure (`--wiki-root-max-width, none`); at 1440px paragraphs run ~140–150 characters per line, double the readable ceiling. The live detector flagged the same paragraphs. This breaks the product's stated reading-first principle on its core task.
   **Why it matters**: readers lose their place on every line return; lookup speed — the whole point — degrades.
   **Fix**: cap prose at ~70ch via a `--wiki-prose-measure` token (default `70ch`) on block children of `.wiki-article-prose`; the card can stay full-width.
   **Suggested command**: /impeccable typeset

2. **[P1] Duplicate H1.** Page-header "Gond" sits ~60px above an in-article H1 "Gond" that is larger and gold — inverted hierarchy, double h1 in the screen-reader outline, and a "did the page load wrong?" beat for first-timers.
   **Why it matters**: every wiki convention (Wikipedia demotes in-body H1s) and WCAG outline hygiene.
   **Fix**: style a leading in-article H1 as visual-h2, or suppress it at render when its text matches the page title.
   **Suggested command**: /impeccable polish

3. **[P1] TOC invisible on desktop.** At 1440px with ~600px of dead margin, Contents hides behind a rotated edge tab and opens as an overlay. Every fluent reference-tool user (Wikipedia, MDN, GitBook) expects persistent section nav when space allows.
   **Why it matters**: recognition-over-recall failure on the reader's primary aid; two interactions for what peers give for free.
   **Fix**: dock the TOC open and sticky in the right margin at ≥1200px (the JS already has the 1199.98px breakpoint in wiki-article-toc.js:199); keep the drawer below that.
   **Suggested command**: /impeccable layout

4. **[P2] Guest FAB dock ships one button.** Logged-out readers get a floating blurred card containing only scroll-to-top, near-colliding with the theme's own bottom-right widgets. For privileged users the same dock stacks up to 8 icon-only buttons (title-attribute tooltips only) — past working-memory limits and invisible to keyboard focus.
   **Fix**: hide the dock when only scroll-to-top would render; for editors, add labels or an overflow menu past 4 actions.
   **Suggested command**: /impeccable distill

5. **[P2] Dead-end article footer + resting glow on the article card.** Content ends at "History" with no sibling/related links (next hop requires scrolling back to the breadcrumb), and the article card carries a `#602044` glow despite not floating — a Floating-Only Rule violation the detector confirmed.
   **Fix**: one quiet "In Lore" sibling-links line under the article; drop the card glow (keep glows on the genuinely floating drawer tabs/FAB if the theme wants them).
   **Suggested command**: /impeccable polish

## Persona Red Flags

**Alex (Power User)**: Two clicks to reach any TOC entry vs zero on Wikipedia; no keyboard shortcut for Contents; when privileged, hover-hunting across up to 8 unlabeled 2.5rem FAB icons to find "Move".

**Sam (Accessibility)**: Double `<h1>` pollutes the heading outline; drawer tabs use `outline: none` with a 28%-alpha box-shadow ring (wiki.css:1076–1083) — likely below 3:1 non-text contrast on the near-black background, verify against WCAG 2.2 Focus Appearance; `title`-only tooltips never surface on keyboard focus.

**Jordan (First-Timer)**: Vertical PAGES/CONTENTS spine tabs read as decorative text, not buttons — nothing signals interactivity until hover; the double title reads as a load error; the vast empty dark region below a short article at 1440px reads as broken layout.

## Minor Observations

- Byline right-aligned while the title is left — provenance floats ~900px from what it describes on wide screens.
- The ~200px decorative rule under the in-article H1 is fixed-width and won't relate to other title lengths.
- Drawer tab `top: clamp(9rem, 34vh, 17rem)` can land below the article card entirely on short pages.
- The gold Search button competes with the page title at first paint and is redundant with Enter.
- Mobile: FAB dock + bottom PAGES/CONTENTS bar + theme chat bubble = three stacked floating layers in the thumb zone.
- Design-system drift backlog (~70 advisory hits) is worth a documentation pass: either promote the recurring literals (`0.875rem`, `#b02a37`, badge radius `999px`) into DESIGN.md/tokens or replace them.
